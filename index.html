Who is Rebel Trading?

Rebel Trading Group was founded by Teddy Teske in 2026 after more than 20 years of trading stocks, options, and intraday scalping systems. The firm was built from real execution experience in live markets—not theory or education.

Our mission is simple: identify disciplined traders, refine their execution, and allocate capital only to those who demonstrate consistent performance under pressure.

Recruitment Process

Rebel Trading operates as a selective trading desk, not a retail education platform. Traders are recruited through performance review rather than subscriptions or paid courses.

To be considered, applicants must submit:
• Trading journal (TradeZella or equivalent)
• Short cover letter describing strategy and experience

Selected traders are assigned to our internal simulation environment and given access to a structured evaluation system.

Sim Evaluation (100K Unified Account)

All approved traders enter the same controlled simulation environment: “Sim Trader 01” — $100,000 TradingView Paper Account

Traders receive structured trade direction and execute within strict risk parameters using real-time market data and simulated execution logic.

Evaluation Rules

• $100,000 simulated capital
• Static drawdown model (no trailing rules)
• Defined daily loss limit
• No time pressure or artificial deadlines

Pass Criteria

• Consistent profitability
• Controlled drawdown behavior
• Repeatable execution (not single trade luck)
• Risk discipline under live conditions

Failure Condition

• Breach of static drawdown limit
• Excessive risk concentration
• Breakdown in execution discipline

Traders who pass the evaluation phase are advanced into a funded simulation environment where performance is tracked under scaled capital conditions.

How It Works

1. Recruitment

Traders submit a journal and cover letter for review by our team.

2. Simulation Assignment

Approved traders receive access to the unified TradingView evaluation account.

3. Performance Evaluation

Traders are assessed on consistency, discipline, and risk management—not just profit.

4. Funded Consideration

Top performers advance into funded simulation and may later be considered for live capital allocation.
